# Competitive Content Analysis Prompt

## Purpose
Generate a content outline by analyzing what competitors have already covered on a topic, then identifying how you can differentiate.

## Process

### Step 1: Competitor Research
Query the RAG databases for competitor coverage on the target topic:
- What has each competitor said about this topic?
- What angles have they taken?
- What questions from their audience remain unanswered?
- What data or claims did they make?

### Step 2: Build Comparison Table

| Question / Subtopic | Competitor 1 Answer | Competitor 2 Answer | Your Answer | Differentiation Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Extracted from RAG] | [From RAG] | [From RAG] | [FILL IN] | [Note unique angle] |

### Step 3: Identify Gaps
- Topics competitors missed entirely
- Data they cited that's now outdated
- Personal experiences you have that competitors don't
- Insider knowledge that only someone in the trenches would have

### Step 4: Hook Development
Formula: **Direct hook + promise + filter + curiosity element**

Pattern options:
- **Popularity + Scarcity**: "[Topic] is the most popular... but most people don't realize [surprising constraint]"
- **Warning**: "Before you [action], there are [number] things nobody talks about"
- **Comparison**: "[Option A] vs [Option B] - which one is actually better for [audience]?"
- **Momentum**: "[Topic] just changed and here's what it means for [audience]"
- **Honest Take**: "I've [credential] and here's what I tell people first"

### Step 5: Outline Structure
1. **Hook** (0:00-0:45): Self-ID question + credibility + roadmap + open loop
2. **Visual CTA #1** (~0:40): Contact info + "Resource in Description"
3. **Content sections**: Each with honest pros/cons structure where applicable
4. **Mid-video CTA** (50-60%): Soft verbal tied to content
5. **Final CTA**: Full verbal + visual
6. **Close**: Return to hook question

## Output Format
- Section-by-section outline with timing estimates
- Key lines in blockquotes (memorize exactly)
- Bullet talking points (speak naturally)
- [VISUAL] tags for on-screen graphics
- Competitive positioning notes per section
- Facts reference sheet (verify with web search)
