# Email Sequence Generation Prompt

## Framework: Russell Brunson's Daily Seinfeld Method

The goal is to write emails that feel like a text from a smart friend in your industry, not a marketing blast. Each email tells a micro-story from real life, pivots to a relevant insight for the reader, and ends with a soft invite.

## Email Structure

### 1. Subject Line
- Curiosity-driven, not promotional
- Reads like a text message
- Industry context when possible
- Examples: "the thing nobody mentions about [topic]", "I almost told them not to [action]", "what I noticed at [event/place] today"

### 2. Opening Line (1-2 sentences)
- Start mid-story (no "Hey [Name], hope you're doing well")
- Pattern interrupt
- Example: "So I'm [doing something relevant] last Thursday and [someone] says something that made me do a double take."

### 3. Micro-Story (100-150 words)
- Real experience or observation from your work
- Show empathy and expertise naturally
- One emotional beat only
- Specific details (names, places, numbers)

### 4. Pivot (25-50 words)
- Bridge from story to reader relevance
- "Here's why this matters if you're thinking about..."
- "The reason I'm telling you this..."

### 5. Soft Invite CTA
- Never hard-sell
- Options: "Just reply [keyword]", "Want me to send you...", "Hit reply if you want the breakdown"
- Make it easy and low-pressure

### 6. Sign-off
- "Talk soon, [Name]" or "- [Name]"

### 7. P.S.
- Specific fact, tip, or upcoming event
- Often the most-read part of the email

## Sequence Types

### Welcome Sequence (5 emails over 10 days)
1. **Day 1**: Origin story — why you do what you do
2. **Day 3**: The #1 mistake people make with [your topic]
3. **Day 5**: Hidden gem or insider insight from your experience
4. **Day 7**: Client/customer pattern story — "most of my [clients] end up..."
5. **Day 10**: Direct value — free resource, consultation, or offer

### Weekly Nurture
- Monday: Weekend observation
- Tuesday: Industry spotlight or deep dive
- Wednesday: Honest pros/cons take
- Thursday: Client pattern or market data
- Friday: Lifestyle or partner recommendation

## Rules
- Max 200 words per email
- Plain text wins (no HTML templates)
- One topic per email
- Real stories only (never fabricated)
- No generic industry jargon
- Include your contact info in every email signature
